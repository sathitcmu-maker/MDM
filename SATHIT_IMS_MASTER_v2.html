7818

PS 7818:2018 — Quality & Food Safety Framework

Internal standard that integrates Codex GHPs/HACCP, ISO 9001:2015 and ISO 22000:2018 into a single operational framework. Acts as the umbrella standard — all food safety and quality requirements are managed through PS 7818 deployment. Simplifies compliance by eliminating duplication across 3 international standards.

Core Concept: PS 7818 is the integration master — it maps requirements from Codex, ISO 9001, and ISO 22000 into one coherent system. Compliance with PS 7818 implies compliance with all three underlying standards simultaneously.

4 Pillars of PS 7818

  • Management System (ISO 9001 base) — PDCA, process approach, risk-based thinking, leadership commitment
  • Food Safety Management (ISO 22000) — FSMS, PRPs, oPRPs, CCPs, HACCP plan
  • Prerequisite Programs (Codex GHPs) — 15 PRP categories covering infrastructure, hygiene, pest, allergen, traceability
  • HACCP System (Codex 2023) — 12 steps, 7 principles, CCP decision tree, monitoring & verification

GHP

Codex Alimentarius — GHPs & HACCP System (2023)

Foundational international food safety framework. GHPs form the prerequisite foundation; HACCP is the risk-based system layered on top. The 2023 revision integrates GHPs and HACCP more explicitly — PRPs are now formally recognized within the HACCP framework.

Core Concept: GHPs = the foundation (control the environment). HACCP = the system (control the process). Without solid GHPs, HACCP cannot function effectively.
PART A — GENERAL HYGIENE PRACTICES (GHPs) — 15 Categories

Infrastructure PRPs

  • Primary production controls
  • Establishment design & layout
  • Equipment design & maintenance
  • Water & air supply quality

Process PRPs

  • Temperature control programs
  • Cleaning & disinfection (C&D)
  • Pest control program
  • Waste & sewage management

Personnel PRPs

  • Personal hygiene standards
  • Health & illness policy
  • Food handler training program
  • Visitor & contractor controls

Product PRPs

  • Allergen management program
  • Traceability system (1 up / 1 down)
  • Recall & withdrawal procedure

GHP vs HACCP Scope

CCP vs oPRP Decision

  • CCP — Hazard significant + no subsequent control + critical limit measurable continuously
  • oPRP — Hazard significant + controlled by operational PRP (not continuous monitoring)
  • PRP — General prerequisite; hazard not significant at this step
  • Decision Tree: Is control essential? → Can it be monitored continuously? → CCP or oPRP

Hazard Categories

  • Biological — Pathogens (Salmonella, Listeria, E.coli O157), spoilage organisms
  • Chemical — Pesticides, veterinary drug residues, mycotoxins, allergens, cleaning agents
  • Physical — Metal, glass, bone, plastic, wood fragments
  • Radiological — (emerging, location-dependent)
  • Significance = Severity × Likelihood

22000

ISO 22000:2018 — Food Safety Management System

Integrates HACCP with the ISO management system framework. Uses HLS structure plus food safety-specific elements: PRPs, oPRPs, HACCP plan, and hazard analysis. PDCA operates at two levels — management system AND HACCP operational level.

Main Concept: ISO 22000 = ISO 9001 structure + Codex HACCP content. Strongest standalone FSMS standard. 2018 revision added clearer PRPs/oPRPs/CCPs distinction and food safety culture requirement.

Dual PDCA Model (ISO 22000 Unique)

  • Outer PDCA — Management system level (same as ISO 9001)
  • Inner PDCA — HACCP operational level (hazard control cycle)
  • Both cycles must be running simultaneously and linked
  • Management review covers both cycles

3 Control Measure Categories

  • PRPs — General hygiene controls; manage general contamination; monitored periodically (GHPs)
  • oPRPs — Operational PRPs; control specific food safety hazards; monitored by procedure
  • CCPs — Critical Control Points; Critical Limits; continuous monitoring; deviation = corrective action

GFSI-benchmarked standard. Issue 9 (2022) focuses on food safety culture, environmental monitoring, and supply chain transparency. Grading: AA / A / B / C / D / Fail. Widely required by UK, EU, and global retailers.

45001

ISO 45001:2018 — Occupational Health & Safety

First international OH&S standard (2018). Replaces OHSAS 18001. Most distinctive requirement: mandatory worker participation and consultation. Safety culture — not just compliance.

Main Concept: Eliminate hazards through proactive prevention — with workers actively involved. Management does not impose safety from above; workers co-own the process.

Hierarchy of Controls (§8.1.2) — Priority Order

  • 1. Elimination — Remove the hazard entirely (highest priority)
  • 2. Substitution — Replace with less hazardous alternative
  • 3. Engineering Controls — Guarding, ventilation, machine isolation
  • 4. Administrative Controls — WIs, training, job rotation, permits
  • 5. PPE — Last resort; lowest priority

Unique: Worker Participation (§5.4)

  • Workers MUST be consulted in hazard identification & risk assessment
  • Workers MUST be involved in incident investigations
  • Workers MUST participate in OH&S policy & objectives development
  • Workers MUST report concerns without fear of reprisal
  • Non-managerial worker representative required

17025

ISO/IEC 17025:2017 — Testing & Calibration Laboratories

Standard for laboratory competence. Not a management standard — a technical competence standard. Accreditation (not certification) granted by national bodies (TISI/NATA/UKAS). Technically distinct from HLS standards.

Main Concept: Demonstrate technical competence to produce valid results. Key concepts: impartiality, measurement traceability, uncertainty of measurement, method validation, proficiency testing.

5 Unique Requirements

  • Impartiality — Lab independent from commercial pressure; conflict of interest declaration
  • Measurement Traceability — All measurements traceable to SI units via calibration chain
  • Measurement Uncertainty — Uncertainty budget calculated and reported per test method
  • Method Validation — Performance characteristics verified before use
  • Proficiency Testing — Interlaboratory comparison; ongoing competence demonstration

5 Requirement Sections

  • General Requirements (§4–5) — Impartiality; confidentiality; legal entity; scope
  • Resource Requirements (§6) — Personnel; equipment; environment; external services
  • Process Requirements (§7) — Method validation; calibration; uncertainty; test reports
  • Management Requirements (§8) — Document control; NCR; data integrity; internal audit; MR
  • Proficiency Testing (§7.7) — Annual PT plan per test method in scope

GAP

Global G.A.P. — IFA v5.2 — Upstream Supply Chain Control

Integrated Farm Assurance standard covering crop, aquaculture, and livestock. Applied in this IMS as upstream control only — ensuring farm suppliers hold valid certification before raw material approval.

Scope: Upstream control only. Verify farm suppliers hold valid Global G.A.P. certification via GGN database. Supplier approval, certificate verification, and audit rights form the control framework.

Key Control Points (KCPs)

  • Food Safety — Water quality; pesticide records; MRL compliance; worker hygiene
  • Traceability — Farm unit to product lot; parallel production control
  • Environment — Biodiversity; soil management; integrated pest management (IPM)
  • Animal Welfare — Five freedoms; transport records
  • Worker H&S — PPE; training; risk assessment; first aid

Upstream Control Framework

  • Supplier Approval — Must hold valid Global G.A.P. certificate before approval
  • Certificate Verification — Check via GLOBALG.A.P. database (GGN number)
  • Annual Renewal Check — Verify at each annual supplier review
  • Suspension Monitoring — Alert system if certificate suspended
  • Audit Rights Clause — Right to audit included in purchase agreement
  • Corrective Action — Supplier CAPA required if certification lapsed

BAP

BAP Farm Standard — Issue 3.1 (Feb 2023) — Upstream Supply Chain Control

Best Aquaculture Practices standard for responsible aquaculture. Issue 3.1 covers shrimp, fish, and other aquatic species. Certified by Global Seafood Alliance (GSA). Applied as upstream control only.

Scope: Upstream control only. Verify aquaculture suppliers hold valid BAP certification. Monitor certificate status. BAP 4-star supply chain preferred where possible.

BAP Issue 3.1 — 5 Core Pillars

  • Food Safety — HACCP; chemical use; pathogen control; residue monitoring
  • Environmental Responsibility — Effluent management; water usage; mangrove protection
  • Social Responsibility — Worker rights; fair wages; no child/forced labor
  • Animal Health & Welfare — Disease management; antibiotic stewardship; biosecurity
  • Traceability — Hatchery → grow-out → processing; lot coding; chain of custody

Upstream Control Framework

  • BAP Certificate Verification — Check via GSA/BAP website before approval
  • Star Rating Preference — 4-star preferred (feed mill + hatchery + farm + processing)
  • Annual Renewal Check — Verify at each annual supplier review
  • Antibiotic Records — Request antibiotic records; no prohibited substances
  • Lot Traceability Test — Verify lot number traces back to certified farm
  • Social Compliance Screening — Self-declaration + on-site when high risk

AUD

Audit Manual

Integrated audit program covering all 10 standards. Multi-standard audit teams assess shared elements once. NCRs graded and linked directly to CAPA. Risk-based frequency.

Audit Philosophy: One audit program — not ten separate audits. Shared elements audited once annually. Standard-specific elements audited per group. Higher risk processes audited more frequently.

Audit Team Competence Requirements

  • Lead Auditor — ISO 19011 trained; at least 2 standards certified; 5+ audit experience
  • QFS Auditor — HACCP certified; food industry background; ISO 22000/9001 knowledge

  • Lab Auditor — ISO 17025 trained; technical laboratory background
  • Independence — Auditors must not audit their own work area
TRN

Training Manual

Training needs matrix, curriculum design, assessment methods, and certificate criteria for all 10 standards. Training organized by role — not by standard — to maximize efficiency and minimize duplication.

Training Philosophy: Awareness training for all staff. Role-specific deep training for those who operate, maintain, or audit the system. Competency assessed — not just attendance recorded.

Assessment & Certification Criteria

  • Awareness Training — Attendance record; post-test ≥ 70% pass mark
  • Role-Specific Training — Written test ≥ 80% + practical demonstration
  • HACCP Team — HACCP study participation + signed HACCP plan
  • Internal Auditor — 16-hr course + witnessed audit + ISO 19011 criteria
  • Certificate — Internal certificate; valid 3 years; refresher required

Annual Training Plan Structure

  • Q1 — Annual GHPs refresher; safety induction (new staff); IMS policy awareness
  • Q2 — HACCP review training; allergen awareness; supplier standard update
  • Q3 — Internal auditor refresher; EHS drills; lab competence verification
  • Q4 — BRCGS pre-audit awareness; management leadership; IMS update briefing
  • On-demand — New hire induction; process change retraining; post-NCR retraining
