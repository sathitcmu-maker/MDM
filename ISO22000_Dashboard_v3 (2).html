ISO 22000:2018 — Framework & Concept
Food Safety Management System — Annex SL High Level Structure | Dual PDCA Model | Core Principles

🏛️ Annex SL Structure

ISO 22000 adopts the common 10-clause Annex SL framework — enabling seamless integration with ISO 9001, ISO 14001 & ISO 45001 through identical clause numbering and shared HLS requirements.

🔄 Dual PDCA Cycle

Unique to ISO 22000: two simultaneous PDCA cycles operate — Organizational level (Clauses 4–10) and Operational level (PRPs → Hazard Analysis → HACCP/OPRP → Verification). Both must be maintained and linked.

⚠️ Risk-Based Thinking

Risk operates at two levels: Organizational risk affecting the FSMS system integrity, and Operational risk (food safety hazards) managed through PRPs, OPRPs and CCPs with defined critical limits.

🌐 Food Chain Scope

Applicable to ALL organizations in the food chain regardless of size — from farming, feed production, food manufacturing, catering, packaging, to transport & storage. Includes feed & animal food producers (2018 addition).

Clause 4 — Context of the Organization

New in ISO 22000:2018 | Establish the strategic foundation before designing the FSMS | Includes climate change consideration

Requirement
Objective
Key Results / Evidence Required
4.1 Understanding the organization and its context
Identify internal & external issues affecting FSMS ability to deliver safe food. Must now also determine whether climate change is a relevant issue.
SWOT AnalysisPESTLEContext Register Updated at Management Review. Minutes or documented analysis retained as evidence.
4.2 Understanding needs & expectations of interested parties
Identify stakeholders (suppliers, regulators, customers, consumers, employees) and their requirements that affect food safety. Note: interested parties may have climate change requirements.
Interested Party RegisterStakeholder Map Requirements mapped and reviewed when changes occur. Regulatory requirements flagged as mandatory.
4.3 Determining the scope of the FSMS
Define physical/geographical boundaries, products/services, and supply chain stages covered. Must reference site location, product types, packaging, shelf-life conditions.
Scope Statement Maintained as documented information. Declared to CB at certification application. On-change review required.
4.4 Food Safety Management System
Establish, implement, maintain and continually improve the FSMS based on outputs of 4.1–4.3. Focus on process interactions within scope.
Process MapIMS Manual All processes and interactions documented. FSMS aligned with organizational strategic direction.

Clause 5 — Leadership

Top management must be present and demonstrably committed | Food Safety Team Leader mandatory | Policy drives the system

Requirement
Objective
Key Results / Evidence Required
5.1 Leadership & Commitment
Top management demonstrates active leadership: resource provision, FSMS integration into business processes, FSMS–strategic plan alignment, management review participation.
Management Review MinutesResource Allocation Records CEO/MD must be interviewed by external auditor. Strategic plan alignment documented.
5.2 Food Safety Policy
Establish a policy appropriate to context. Must commit to: framework for objectives, applicable requirements, internal/external communication, continual improvement, food safety competencies.
Food Safety Policy Document Signed by CEO. Communicated to all staff, suppliers on request. Posted on noticeboards, induction packs. Reviewed annually by top management.
5.3 Roles, Responsibilities & Authorities
Define and communicate responsibilities across all levels. ISO 22000 specifically requires a nominated Food Safety Team Leader with authority to manage the FST, ensure FSMS effectiveness, and report to top management.
Org ChartJob DescriptionsFST Leader Appointment Letter All duties and authorities documented. Food Safety Team composition and roles defined.

Clause 6 — Planning

Action planning for risks & opportunities | SMART food safety objectives | Planned change management

Requirement
Objective
Key Results / Evidence Required
6.1 Actions to address risks & opportunities
Address organizational-level risks (not just food hazards). Distinguish recurrent events (handled by corrective action) from sporadic systemic risks. Actions must be proportionate to potential impact on food safety.
Risk RegisterOpportunity RegisterAction Plan Plan includes: what, resources, responsible person, completion date, evaluation method.
6.2 Food Safety Objectives
Set measurable objectives consistent with Food Safety Policy. Must be: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Timely (SMART). Monitored and communicated to all staff.
Objectives MatrixKPI Dashboard Objectives documented. Progress tracked periodically. Displayed at site. Updated when context changes.
6.3 Planning of Changes
When FSMS changes are needed (products, processes, regulatory), conduct planned change review: extent of change, impact on existing system, resourcing, effect on roles/responsibilities.
Change Management FormFSMS Update Record Change review documented before implementation. HACCP re-validation triggered for significant changes.

Clause 7 — Support

Resources · Competence · Awareness · Communication · Documented Information — the underpinning infrastructure of FSMS

Requirement
Objective
Key Results / Evidence Required
7.1 Resources
Determine and provide all resources: people, infrastructure (premises, equipment, software, transport, storage), work environment, externally developed FSMS elements, externally provided processes/products/services.
Equipment RegisterMaintenance RecordsSupplier Agreements Work environment conditions documented (temperature, humidity, hygiene requirements).
7.2 Competence
Ensure all personnel affecting food safety are competent. Identify training gaps, provide training, evaluate effectiveness. Competence records mandatory including for external providers on-site.
Training MatrixTraining RecordsCompetence Assessment Records signed by employee. Refresher training scheduled. Trainer qualifications evidenced.
7.3 Awareness
All personnel must understand the Food Safety Policy, their contribution to FSMS effectiveness, and consequences of not complying with FSMS requirements.
Induction RecordsToolbox Talk Records Awareness addressed at recruitment, induction, appraisals, regular meetings.
7.4 Communication
Establish effective internal and external communication systems. Plan: What · Who communicates · When · To whom · How. External: suppliers, customers, regulators, CB. Internal: food safety team, all personnel.
Communication PlanExternal Communication Records Management review uses communication outputs as input. Contractors treated as direct employees for communication purposes.
7.5 Documented Information
Maintain (keep current) and retain (keep as evidence) documented information as required by the standard. Documents must be: identifiable, in suitable format, protected, available to right people at right time.
Document Master ListDocument Control Procedure Version control, revision history, review frequency, retention period and disposal method all defined.

Clause 8 — Operation

The core of FSMS — where all HACCP principles integrate | PRPs → Hazard Analysis → Control Plan → Verification | All mandatory clauses

Requirement
Objective
Key Results / Evidence Required
8.1 Operational Planning & Control
Plan, implement, control and maintain all processes for product/service requirements, food safety objectives, and PRPs/HACCP plan. Outsourced processes must be identified and controlled.
Process Control DocumentsProduction Records All controls documented. Externally provided processes specifications established before agreement.
8.2 Prerequisite Programmes (PRPs)
Establish PRPs appropriate to context using ISO/TS 22002 series. Must cover: construction, layout, utilities, waste, cleaning, pest control, personnel hygiene, supplier management, cross-contamination prevention.
PRP DocumentsPRP Monitoring RecordsVerification Records PRPs established BEFORE hazard analysis. Selection, implementation, monitoring and verification documented.
8.3 Traceability System
Ensure traceability of all lots from raw material receipt through finished product distribution. System must be challenged/tested. Retention period not less than shelf-life of product.
Traceability ProcedureLot Coding SystemTraceability Exercise Records ISO 22005 as reference. Forward and backward trace to lot level within defined timeframe.
8.4 Emergency Preparedness & Response
Establish procedures for potential emergency situations (natural disasters, contamination, sabotage, utility failure). Must be tested — this is now crystal clear in 2018 version. Test records retained.
Emergency Response ProcedureMock Recall RecordsDrill Records Minimum annual test. Results documented. Procedures updated based on test outcomes.
8.5 Hazard Analysis & Control (HACCP Core)
Multidisciplinary Food Safety Team → Preliminary info gathering → Flow diagram verification → Hazard identification → Hazard assessment (severity × probability) → Control measure selection → CCP/OPRP categorization → Validation → Hazard Control Plan → Monitoring → Corrections/CAs.
FST ListProduct DescriptionFlow DiagramHazard Analysis WorksheetHACCP PlanValidation RecordsMonitoring RecordsDeviation Records
8.7 Control of Monitoring & Measuring
Ensure measuring equipment is fit for purpose, calibrated at specified intervals. Equipment list maintained. Results traceable to national/international measurement standards.
Calibration RegisterCalibration RecordsEquipment List Out-of-calibration procedure defined. Impact on previous results assessed when found out of calibration.
8.9 Control of Nonconforming Products
When CCP limits or OPRP action criteria exceeded: identify, isolate, evaluate, and disposition. Cannot release without documented authorization. Withdrawal/recall triggered if product left premises.
Nonconforming Product ProcedureHold TagsDisposition RecordsRecall Procedure Corrections and CAs documented. Staff authorized for these activities are competent.

Clause 9 — Performance Evaluation

Three-pillar evaluation: Process monitoring · Internal Audit · Management Review | All feed into continual improvement

Requirement
Objective
Key Results / Evidence Required
9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis & Evaluation
Determine what to monitor/measure, methods, frequency, who analyses results, when results reported. KPIs directly linked to food safety objectives (§6.2). Results used as input to management review and FSMS updating.
KPI ReportPerformance DashboardTrend Analysis Retained as documented information. Compliance evaluation documented quarterly at minimum.
9.2 Internal Audit
Planned audit programme covering all FSMS processes. Risk-based frequency: high-risk processes audited more frequently. Auditors must not audit their own work. Findings reported to FST and management. Corrections/CAs without undue delay.
Annual Audit ScheduleAudit ChecklistsAudit ReportsCAPA Log Programme considers previous results and changes affecting processes. All clauses 4-10 covered across audit cycle.
9.3 Management Review
Top management reviews FSMS effectiveness, adequacy and suitability. 2018 adds new inputs: context changes, climate change, provider performance trends, risk/opportunity effectiveness. Minimum annual. More frequent = better system health.
Management Review AgendaManagement Review MinutesAction Register Outputs: CI opportunities, FSMS changes needed, resource decisions. Minutes retained as documented information.

Clause 10 — Improvement

System-level nonconformities (not just operational) | Structured CAPA methodology | Continual improvement as an outcome of full FSMS implementation

Requirement
Objective
Key Results / Evidence Required
10.1 Nonconformity & Corrective Action
React → Control & correct → Deal with consequences → Evaluate root cause → Determine if similar NCs exist elsewhere → Implement CA → Review CA effectiveness → Update FSMS if necessary. Now covers all management system nonconformities, not just operational.
CAR FormCAPA LogRoot Cause Analysis Records Log tracks: date, NC description, responsibility, due date, action taken, closure date. Trend coding enables pattern analysis at Management Review.
10.2 Continual Improvement
Organization shall continually improve suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of the FSMS. Improvement occurs as the natural output of correctly implementing all Clauses 4-10 including FSMS updating.
Improvement RegisterInnovation Log Projects linked to KPI gaps. Resources assigned. Effectiveness measured against original objective.
10.3 Update of the FSMS
Top management ensures resources for continual FSMS updating. Food Safety Team evaluates FSMS at planned intervals considering: hazard analysis currency, PRP/HACCP plan relevance, communication outputs, verification results, management review outputs.
FSMS Update RecordHazard Analysis Review Update activities retained as documented information. Reported as input to next management review.

    🎯 Top 9 Success Factors for FSMS Implementation
    ① Top Management Commitment
    CEO must be actively involved — not just a signature on the policy. External auditor WILL interview top management directly.
    ② Multidisciplinary FST
    Food Safety Team must span QA, Production, Engineering, Procurement. Diverse knowledge = better hazard identification.
    ③ Dual PDCA Discipline
    Both organizational and operational PDCA cycles must function independently and in communication with each other at all times.
    ④ Hazard-Specific Controls
    Food safety hazards are process and product specific — not interchangeable between products. Each product/process combination analyzed independently.
    ⑤ Change Management
    Planned or unintentional product/process changes must trigger FSMS review. HACCP re-validation required for significant changes.
    ⑥ Calibration Discipline
    All monitoring equipment used for CCP/OPRP control must be calibrated at defined frequency. Out-of-calibration protocol critical.
    ⑦ Supplier Risk Awareness
    High-risk suppliers must have controls at least as strong as yours. Approved supplier list with risk-tiered evaluation and monitoring.
    ⑧ Personnel Competence Culture
    Food safety concepts require habit change. Single awareness briefing insufficient. Ongoing competence program with effectiveness evaluation mandatory.
    ⑨ Test Controls Routinely
    Threats constantly evolve. Traceability exercises, mock recalls, emergency drills must be conducted and results used to improve systems.

    4
    DOCUMENTATION / PROCESS DESIGN
    Write FSMS Manual, all procedures, HACCP plan, PRP documents, WIs and all forms (Tier 1-4). Review with process owners. Establish document control system before issuing.
    5

    Roll out all procedures across all departments. Train all staff. Begin generating records as evidence. Start CCP/OPRP monitoring, calibration and PRP monitoring programmes.
    6
    INTERNAL AUDIT
    Conduct first internal audit covering all clauses 4-10. Issue audit reports. Implement corrective actions for all NCs and verify effectiveness before moving to certification stage.
    7
    ORGANISE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM REVIEW MEETING
    Top management reviews all §9.3 inputs: policy, objectives, context changes, audit results, verification results, HACCP plan currency, provider performance, legal compliance, resource adequacy. Formally minuted.

    🔍 1st Party — Internal Audit

    Conducted by organization's own trained internal auditors. Auditors must not audit their own work area. Annual programme at minimum — high-risk processes audited more frequently. All clauses 4–10 covered across full audit cycle. Findings reported to FST and management. Primary purpose: system improvement and pre-certification preparation.

    🤝 2nd Party — Supplier Audit

    Organization audits its own suppliers, particularly those supplying high-risk raw materials or outsourced processes. Based on supplier risk tier from approved supplier programme. Results feed supplier approval status and purchasing decisions. Focuses on FSMS controls, PRPs, hazard controls, and regulatory compliance at supplier site.

    🏛️ 3rd Party — Certification Audit

    Stage 1: CB auditor reviews documentation — scope, FSMS Manual, HACCP plan, PRPs, objective evidence. Usually off-site or brief on-site. Stage 2: Full on-site audit against all ISO 22000 requirements. Surveillance audits annually. Recertification every 3 years. Top management must participate — no exceptions.

    Full Audit Cycle — PDCA-Based Approach
    Phase 1 — Plan

    📋 Audit Planning

    • Define audit scope, objectives, and criteria
    • Select auditors (competent, impartial — not own area)
    • Risk-based frequency determination
    • Develop annual audit programme
    • Consider previous audit results and process changes
    • Prepare audit notification to auditee
    • Allocate adequate time and auditor skills
    Phase 2 — Do

    🔎 Audit Execution

    • Opening meeting: scope, method, logistics
    • Document review: procedures, records, HACCP plan
    • On-site observation: PRPs, CCP monitoring, process controls
    • Interview personnel at all levels including top management
    • Sampling and testing record review
    • Traceability exercise performance
    • Collect objective evidence (photos, records, data)
    • Closing meeting: present findings, classify NCs
    Phase 3 — Check

    📝 Reporting & CAPA

    • Issue formal audit report within agreed timeframe
    • Classify findings: Major NC / Minor NC / Observation / OFI
    • Auditee acknowledges findings
    • Root cause analysis for each NC
    • CAPA plan developed with target dates
    • Correction (immediate fix) separated from CA (systemic fix)
    • CAs submitted to auditor/programme manager
    Phase 4 — Act

    ✅ Verification & Improvement

    • Auditor verifies CA effectiveness at agreed date
    • Close NCs when evidence confirms effectiveness
    • Audit results reported to management
    • Inputs fed to Management Review (§9.3)
    • Audit programme updated based on findings
    • Systemic improvements identified across other processes
    • Auditor competence reviewed and updated
    🔬 FSMS-Specific Audit Focus Areas
    PRPs Audit
    • Cleaning schedule compliance and verification records
    • Pest control sighting trends and corrective actions
    • Personnel hygiene practices observed on-site
    • Environmental monitoring programme and results
    • Allergen segregation and cross-contamination controls
    • Supplier approval status and evaluation records
    HACCP Plan Audit
    • Flow diagram matches actual process (on-site verified)
    • Hazard analysis completeness and scientific justification
    • CCP/OPRP categorization rationale documented
    • Critical limits — measurable and validated
    • Monitoring records complete, no gaps, signed off
    • Deviations handled per procedure — corrections documented
    • Validation records for all control measures
    Traceability & Emergency Audit
    • Traceability exercise — conduct live trace forward + backward
    • Exercise completion time within target
    • 100% quantity reconciliation of traced lots
    • Recall procedure tested and records available
    • Emergency contact list current and accessible
    • Previous drill findings addressed and improved
    🔴 Major NC
    Complete absence of a required system element, or a critical food safety risk. Immediate CAPA required. Certification held until closed.
    🟠 Minor NC
    Partial compliance with a requirement. System element exists but has gaps. CAPA required within agreed timeframe. Certification may proceed.
    🟡 Observation
    Not yet a NC but trending toward one. No formal CA required but organization should monitor and address proactively.
    🟢 OFI
    Opportunity for Improvement. Beyond compliance — good practice suggestion from auditor. Organization's discretion to implement.
