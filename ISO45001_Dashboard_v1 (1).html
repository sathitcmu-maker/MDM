ISO 45001:2018 — Framework & Concept
Occupational Health & Safety Management System — PDCA Cycle | Annex SL High Level Structure | Core OH&S Principles

🏗️ Annex SL Structure

ISO 45001 adopts the common 10-clause Annex SL (HLS) framework — enabling seamless integration with ISO 9001:2015 and ISO 14001:2015. Replaces OHSAS 18001 as the primary international OH&S standard.

🔄 PDCA Cycle

Plan-Do-Check-Act is the operating engine of ISO 45001. Applied at both system and process level. Top Management is the core driver at every stage, ensuring OH&S is embedded in business strategy — not treated as an add-on.

⚠️ Risk-Based Thinking

Hazard identification and OH&S risk assessment are fundamental. The Hierarchy of Controls (Eliminate → Substitute → Engineer → Admin → PPE) guides risk reduction to a reasonably practicable level across all operations.

👷 Worker Participation

A key differentiator of ISO 45001: consultation and participation of workers (§5.4) is a standalone requirement. Workers must be involved in hazard identification, risk assessment, incident investigation, and OH&S objective setting.

Clause 4 — Context of the Organization

Establish strategic foundation | Identify internal/external issues | Define scope | Climate change consideration added

Requirement
Objective
Key Results / Evidence Required
4.1 Understanding the organization and its context
Identify internal & external issues that affect OH&S system outcomes. Determine whether climate change is a relevant issue. Review at planned intervals and when significant changes occur.
Context RegisterSWOT/PESTLEIssue Log Documented analysis retained. Updated at Management Review. Climate change relevance determined and recorded.
4.2 Needs & expectations of workers and other interested parties
Identify workers and stakeholders who can affect or be affected by OH&S decisions. Determine their relevant requirements. Some requirements become compliance obligations (legal/regulatory).
Interested Party RegisterStakeholder MapCompliance Obligations List Workers listed explicitly. Requirements reviewed when changes occur.
4.3 Determining the scope of the OH&S management system
Define boundaries — activities, locations, workers covered. Cannot exclude areas to avoid OH&S obligations. Scope must account for 4.1 & 4.2 outputs and organizational control.
Scope Statement Available as documented information. Declared to CB at certification. Areas excluded must be justifiable. On-change review required.
4.4 OH&S management system
Establish, implement, maintain and continually improve the OH&S MS including its processes and their interactions per this standard.
OH&S MS Manual/FrameworkProcess Map All processes and interactions documented. System integrated into business processes.

Clause 5 — Leadership & Worker Participation

Top management must be champions — not delegates | OH&S Policy | Worker consultation is a standalone requirement

Requirement
Objective
Key Results / Evidence Required
5.1 Leadership and commitment
Top management must take overall accountability for OH&S outcomes — not delegate. Must integrate OH&S into business processes, promote culture, protect workers from reprisal when reporting incidents.
Leadership EvidenceSafety Committee RecordsManagement Walk-Throughs Auditor will interview top management directly. Evidence of active participation required.
5.2 OH&S policy
Signed by CEO. Must commit to: safe & healthy workplaces, hazard elimination, legal compliance, worker consultation, continual improvement. Must be communicated to all workers and available to interested parties.
Signed OH&S PolicyCommunication RecordsDisplay Evidence Policy displayed, included in induction. Reviewed periodically for continued suitability.
5.3 Roles, responsibilities and authorities
Define and communicate OH&S roles throughout the organization. Top management retains ultimate responsibility. Each role must have documented responsibilities for their OH&S area.
Org Chart with OH&S RolesJob DescriptionsAuthority Matrix Communicated to all workers. Reviewed when structure changes.
5.4 Consultation and participation of workers
Establish, implement and maintain process for worker consultation and participation. Workers must be involved in: hazard ID, risk assessment, incident investigation, objective setting, NC identification. Non-managerial workers must participate in all OH&S decisions.
Safety Committee MinutesToolbox Talk RecordsWorker Feedback SystemNear-Miss Reports Documented process. Evidence that workers at all levels are engaged.

Clause 6 — Planning

Risk-based planning | Hazard identification is the foundation | Legal register mandatory | SMART objectives required

Requirement
Objective
Key Results / Evidence Required
6.1.1 General — Actions to address risks & opportunities
Determine OH&S risks, OH&S opportunities, other risks and other opportunities. Plan actions proportionate to impact on OH&S performance. Consider organizational risks from 4.1 and interested party requirements from 4.2.
Risk & Opportunity RegisterAction Plan Risks rated and prioritized. Opportunities for improvement documented. Linked to objectives.
6.1.2 Hazard identification and risk assessment
Systematic, proactive, ongoing process. Must consider: routine/non-routine activities, emergency situations, human factors, infrastructure, contractor activities, design changes. Apply Hierarchy of Controls. Workers must participate.
Hazard RegisterRisk AssessmentsHIRA MatrixWorker Sign-off All significant hazards documented. Risk scores assigned. Control measures specified and implemented.
6.1.3 Determination of legal and other requirements
Establish, implement and maintain process to determine all applicable legal requirements (national/local OH&S law, regulations) and other requirements (industry standards, customer requirements, collective agreements).
Legal RegisterCompliance Obligations List Updated at defined frequency. Source identified. Compliance status tracked. Linked to risk assessments.
6.1.4 Planning action
Plan how to integrate and implement actions addressing risks, opportunities and legal requirements into OH&S management system processes. Evaluate effectiveness of these actions.
Action PlanIntegration Evidence Actions embedded in operational procedures, not standalone lists. Effectiveness reviewed at management review.
6.2 OH&S objectives and planning to achieve them
Set SMART OH&S objectives consistent with OH&S policy. Must be measurable, monitored, communicated, updated as needed. Planning must specify: what/who/when/how/how evaluated. Resources identified.
Objectives MatrixKPI DashboardPerformance Data Objectives tracked and reported to management review. Trend analysis maintained.

    ISO 45001:2018 — Audit Guideline
    Full-cycle audit process | 1st, 2nd & 3rd party audits | Tools & checklists for OH&S system assessment
    📋 GAP ANALYSIS CHECK LIST ✅ READINESS CHECK LIST
    🔍
    ISO 45001 uses Risk-Based Audit thinking: allocate more audit time to higher-risk activities, areas with previous NCs, and processes where human factors are critical. Worker participation in the audit process is a key requirement — workers should be interviewed, not just managers.
    AUDIT CYCLE Full Audit Process — Phase by Phase
    Phase 1

    📅 Audit Planning

    • Develop annual audit programme (all clauses 4–10)
    • Risk-based frequency: higher risk = more frequent
    • Select impartial auditors (not own work area)
    • Determine audit criteria and scope per audit
    • Prepare clause-based audit checklists
    • Notify auditees and agree logistics
    • Review previous audit findings before audit
    Phase 2

    🚀 Audit Conduct

    • Opening meeting: scope, criteria, method, logistics
    • Document review: all mandatory documented information
    • On-site observation: actual OH&S controls in practice
    • Interview workers at ALL levels (not just managers)
    • Walk-through: hazard identification, housekeeping, PPE use
    • Test emergency systems: alarms, exits, first aid
    • Review records: training, incidents, inspections, CAPA
    Phase 3

    📝 Audit Reporting

    • Classify findings: NC Major / NC Minor / OFI / Observation
    • Closing meeting: present findings to auditee
    • Issue formal audit report within agreed timeframe
    • NC descriptions: reference clause, objective evidence
    • Distribute report to relevant management
    • Enter all NCs in CAPA register
    • Assign NC owners with target closure dates
    Phase 4

    ✅ CAPA & Closure

    • Root cause analysis for each NC (5-Why, fishbone)
    • Corrective action development — address root cause
    • Implement CA with timeline and resource allocation
    • Submit evidence of CA to audit lead
    • Verify effectiveness before closing NC
    • Update audit programme with lessons learned
    • Report results to management review
    AUDIT TYPES 1st, 2nd & 3rd Party Audits

    1st Party — Internal Audit

    Conducted by organization's own trained auditors. Must cover all clauses 4–10 annually. Auditors must not audit their own work areas. Risk-based programme — higher risk = more frequent. Results drive management review agenda. Unplanned "walk-through" audits recommended in addition to planned programme.

    2nd Party — External / Customer Audit

    Conducted by customers, regulatory authorities, or organizations on their behalf. No advance notice required from regulators — emphasizes need for OH&S readiness at all times. Provides independent view of OH&S performance. Findings drive CAPA. Results strengthen business relationships and demonstrate compliance commitment.

    3rd Party — Certification Audit

    Conducted by UKAS/IAF-accredited certification bodies. Stage 1 (document review) → Stage 2 (on-site assessment). Auditor determines audit-days based on scope, risk, and employee count. Certification valid 3 years with annual surveillance audits. Major NCs = CAPA required before certificate issued. Recertification every 3 years.
